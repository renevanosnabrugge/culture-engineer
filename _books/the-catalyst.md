Pushing harder rarely changes minds. Catalysts remove the barriers preventing people from changing themselves.

Berger's insight is that change agents typically try to add force — more arguments, more evidence, more urgency. Catalysts work differently: they identify what is preventing change and remove it. The result requires less effort and produces more durable change.

## The REDUCE framework

**R — Reactance**: People resist when they feel pushed. Give them autonomy, let them feel like the decision is theirs.

**E — Endowment**: People overvalue what they already have. Help them see the cost of the status quo, not just the benefit of the new.

**D — Distance**: Ask people to change too much at once, and they reject the premise entirely. Start with smaller asks.

**U — Uncertainty**: The unknown feels risky. Reduce uncertainty through trials, demos, and evidence.

**C — Corroborating Evidence**: Some beliefs require more than one data point to shift. Identify the right sources.

## Why I use this framework

In platform adoption and digital transformation, the failure mode is almost always on the demand side, not the supply side. The platform is built. The training is done. And adoption is stalling. REDUCE helps diagnose *which specific barrier* is causing resistance — which is a very different question from "how do we push harder?"
