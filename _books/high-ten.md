Culture is not what a team has. It is what a team does, consistently, when no one is watching. And it is almost entirely the product of deliberate leadership behaviour.

Martin Rooney — best known for his work with elite athletes and coaches — argues that the principles that build championship sports teams apply directly to any team. The book gives leaders a practical system for creating the kind of culture that people are genuinely proud to be part of.

## Core ideas

**The Ten** — ten daily behaviours that, practised consistently, build a great team culture. They are not complicated. They are not heroic. They are repeatable actions that compound over time.

**Culture is a verb** — teams don't have culture; they do culture. The behaviours that show up daily, in how people greet each other, handle disagreement, and respond to mistakes, are the culture.

**The leader is the thermostat** — the team's culture is set by the leader's behaviour. Not the mission statement. Not the values poster. The behaviour.

## Why I find this valuable

It is the most practically actionable book I know on building team culture. It does not get lost in theory. It gives you specific things to do starting tomorrow. The sports context is a feature, not a limitation — elite athletic teams have had to solve the culture problem in very visible, measurable ways, and the patterns that emerge are directly transferable.
