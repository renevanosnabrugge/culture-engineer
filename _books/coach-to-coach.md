Great coaches focus on developing people, not just performance. The best coaches produce great human beings first, and high performers second. The performance usually follows.

Rooney delivers this through a conversation between two coaches across several decades — a mentor passing on what he has learned about what coaching actually requires. The format is simple. The content is practical and occasionally uncomfortable.

## Key ideas

**Coaching is a relationship** — you cannot coach someone who does not trust you, and you cannot build trust without genuinely caring about the person. The tactical stuff comes later.

**The coach's job is to release potential, not install skills** — most coaches approach their role as teachers. The more effective approach is to understand what is already there and remove what is blocking it.

**Culture before performance** — a team that performs well under pressure is a team with a strong culture. The performance is an output. Building the culture is the input.

## What I got out of this book.

I really liked the story of this one. Of how simple things. simple rituals, can already make a team. I like it because Engineering managers or leaders are coaches too. Whether they use the word or not. The habits Rooney describes, showing genuine interest in the person, giving feedback with care, holding people to high standards while supporting them through failure, are exactly what distinguishes engineering leaders who retain great people from those who don't.
