Workplace unhappiness, frustration, and underperformance are usually not character flaws. They are mismatches between the work you're doing and the type of work you're wired for. Lencioni identifies six genius types that map onto the lifecycle of any project.

## The six types

1. **Wonder** — generating questions, identifying opportunities, sensing that something needs to change.
2. **Invention** — creating solutions to problems, generating original ideas.
3. **Discernment** — evaluating ideas, providing instinctive feedback about what will work.
4. **Galvanising** — rallying people around an idea, creating energy and enthusiasm.
5. **Enablement** — providing support, helping others execute, removing obstacles.
6. **Tenacity** — ensuring completion, pushing through resistance, finishing.

Every project needs all six. Most teams are missing at least one. The project typically stalls wherever the gap is.

## Why this model is useful

It reframes common team frustrations productively. The person who always asks "but why are we doing this?" is not being obstructive — they have a Wonder genius. The person who won't let go of a detail has Tenacity. The person who gets the team excited but then disappears from execution has Galvanising without Tenacity.

Understanding your team's genius profile helps assign work more effectively and explains why certain handoffs consistently break down.
