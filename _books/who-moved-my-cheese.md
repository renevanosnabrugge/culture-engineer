A short parable about four characters — two mice (Sniff and Scurry) and two Littlepeople (Hem and Haw) — living in a maze and searching for cheese. When the cheese disappears, their reactions differ dramatically.

Sniff and Scurry notice the change early and adapt immediately. Hem refuses to accept that the cheese is gone and demands it back. Haw eventually overcomes his fear, ventures into the maze, and discovers new cheese — along with some hard-won wisdom written on the walls.

## The core insight

Clinging to what was is far more dangerous than moving toward what could be. Change is constant. The question is only whether you notice it early or late, and whether you adapt or resist.

## Why a book this simple is worth recommending

I have worked with organisations where highly intelligent, experienced people behaved exactly like Hem — demanding that the old cheese come back, refusing to see that the environment had changed. The parable is simple enough to be slightly uncomfortable if you find yourself in it.

It's a good opener for a change management conversation, especially with people who intellectually accept that change is necessary but emotionally are not ready to move. The story provides a shared vocabulary without the defensiveness that direct feedback sometimes triggers.
