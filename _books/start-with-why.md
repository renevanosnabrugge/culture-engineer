Great leaders and great organisations communicate from the inside out — starting with why, then how, then what. Most communicate from the outside in: here's what we do, here's how we do it, and if you're still listening, here's why.

Sinek calls this the Golden Circle. The Why is the purpose, cause, or belief that drives everything. The How is the process. The What is the output. Leaders who inspire — Apple, Southwest Airlines, Martin Luther King — lead with why and let the what follow.

## The Golden Circle in practice

**Most organisations**: "We make computers. They're beautifully designed and easy to use. Want to buy one?"

**Apple**: "Everything we do, we believe in challenging the status quo. We believe in thinking differently. The way we challenge the status quo is by making our products beautifully designed and easy to use. We just happen to make computers. Want to buy one?"

The message is the same. The sequence is inverted. The effect is completely different.

## Why this applies to engineering leadership

Engineering leaders who lead with Why — who articulate what the work is *for*, not just what the deliverable is — build more committed teams. Engineers, in particular, are highly sensitive to purpose misalignment. A team that understands why a platform matters builds it differently than a team executing a specification.

This also applies to how technology change is sold internally. Boards and executives who understand the Why behind a platform modernisation are better partners than those who only see the budget line.
