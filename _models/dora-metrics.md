DORA Metrics are four measures of software delivery performance, backed by six years of research across thousands of organisations. They are the most widely validated leading indicators of technology team health available.

## The four metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Elite benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| **Deployment Frequency** | How often code is deployed to production | Multiple times per day |
| **Lead Time for Changes** | Time from code commit to production | Less than 1 hour |
| **Change Failure Rate** | Percentage of deployments causing incidents | 0–15% |
| **Mean Time to Restore** | Time to recover from a production failure | Less than 1 hour |

## What the research shows

High performers on DORA Metrics consistently show:
- Higher organisational performance
- Better employee satisfaction and engagement
- Lower burnout rates
- Greater ability to deliver commercial and non-commercial goals

The correlation holds across industries, company sizes, and geographies. There are no high performers on cultural health who are low performers on DORA, and vice versa.

## Why these four specifically

The first two (Deployment Frequency, Lead Time) measure throughput — the ability to get value to customers quickly. The second two (Change Failure Rate, Mean Time to Restore) measure stability — the ability to maintain reliability while moving fast.

Most organisations optimise for one at the expense of the other. Elite performers achieve high throughput *and* high stability simultaneously. That is only possible with strong engineering culture, automated testing, and psychological safety to surface problems early.

## How I use this model

DORA Metrics translate engineering culture into board-level language. Deployment frequency and lead time are delivery predictability metrics. Change failure rate and MTTR are operational risk metrics. Boards can understand these. They belong in board reporting alongside financial and commercial metrics.
